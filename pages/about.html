Sovereign is all about Balance.

There are a few ways to lose, but a whole bunch of factors that can lead to them.

Ways you can LOSE:

            Starving to death!

            Invading army kills everyone!

            Your citizen's kick you out because satisfaction is less then 20%!

Best to avoid any of those happening.

Click the Buttons Below to Find out How to Not Lose!

Full Farms means Full Bellies

Food is pretty easy to come by.

Your only outsource is the citizen needs, which is one per month per citizen.

Hunting for your food seems pretty ideal.

However famines are a possiblity.

Famines:

            Farms make 30% less.

            Citizens need x2 as much food.

            Famines last 3 months.

Because of this uncontrolable event, it's best to keep farms well staffed.

            Additionally Robbers have a chance of taking food.

            And lost invasions will take food as well.

BE PREPARED

Invasions start happening after you go past 30 citizens.

The enemy strength is determined by your population.

The more people you have, the more you should invest in defences.

Ways to Defend

            Weapons allow regular citizens to fight.

            Soldiers cost a monthly wage.

            Archers cost a monthly wage.

            Catapults require two soldiers to man and wages.

            Build up walls for big defensive boosts.

            Hire Mercinaries to be safe for three months.

Should you lose an invasion, you're kingdom is ransacked of its resources.

Deaths are calculated by how bad you lost.

Better defenses mean less deaths.

After every invasion, soldiers, archers and catapults are the first to die.

So remember to restrengthen after every battle!

Happy town!

Satisfaction is based on a bunch of differenct factors.

Some of them are out of your control, so keep them happy!

Ways to HAPPY

            Hold feasts or town faires for temporary boosts.

            The Market, Town Square and Tavern give perminant boosts.

            Keep those taxes low!

            Be victorious in battle for boosts! Lose and citizens will be sad.

            Certain Special Items help with satisfaction.

Disasters, bad special items, high taxes, losing to invasions are all things that could get you booted from town.

Cheat code is available to disable death by satisfaction.

Rad Strats

You've come this far to figure out how to play the game, now I'll share with you how to break it.

Not completly true of course, but I feel since the current highscores have already set such a high bar, you deserve to know the secrets.

I would patch the following methods, but that'd essentially make the highest scores impossible to beat.

Not only did the people who set them sit down for a long period of time to set them, but they played long enough to realize and exploit the subtle details they found.

They're dedication alone stands by their highscores, but you deserve a shot too right?

Here are the secrets...

1. The Arms Dealer

Buying and selling from the market was intentionally made to seem expensive, overpriced items encourages you avoid purchasing unless need arrises.

Late game lumber and other resources become irrelevant and stocked high, so it offers a way to exchange that into something useful again.

I set the price for weapons high assuming it would be futile to use unless you completely lacked stone.

However the game uses the same worth values for buying and selling. Therefore you can make weapons CHEAP and sell them for LOTS of gold.

This allows you to turn small amounts of gold and stone, into large amounts of other resources.

Match that with the Wizard's Spell and you have chance of exploding your resources full of enough to last you years.

The game tracks your average actions per month, which is logged with your high score. If you take a look at the highest scores, you'll notice they're rediculously high.

It's a funny downside to the tactic. The market has a multiplyer in which you can sell in larger quantities, weapons does not.

You want to break the high score? Break they're button presses per month in buying and selling weapons.

2. The Underwhelming Villiage

Sovereign has a few ways of determining how much bad luck to throw your way.

The first of which is a short 3 month grace period at the start to allow you to begin to build up.

Simply put, satisfaction depressions are lowered to allow you to focus completely on your small stockpile of resources.

The second is unavoidable. Sovereign begins to add to the chance of invasions and disasters each month after your first year.

Invasions and Disasters happen every 20-25 days, in which you have a CHANCE of either. In late game your chance of such events is 100%.

The last way, which IS exploitable, is by population.

Sovereign won't bash you right after a huge loss of citizens, and does that by checking your population.

Invasions themselves can be game breaking, but only occur after your population grows past 30 people. Additionally they're strength is determined by your population.

You'll notice some of the highscores have low max populations, because they decided to stay low.

Obvious downside is the complete lack of labor. The difficulty of the game increases anyways overtime.

However with that low of a population, you can focus your labor on resources and depend on hunting for food.

Might not seem like the grandest way to run your empire, but the end game is survival.

3. The Dictator

Gold solves all of your issues.

Get enough and you'll be fine no matter what.

Taxes will lower your satisfaction but with enough money to buy food, a stream of feasts and faires will keep you afloat.

With enough gold, some of the special market items are cruel, but offer high rewards.

Taking advantage of them requires a high amount of gold.

This method is more useful mid or late game, but can have long lasting results.
